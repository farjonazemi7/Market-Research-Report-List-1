<p><p>The aluminum silicon alloy market encompasses three primary types: casting, extrusion, and forging. Casting involves pouring molten alloy into molds to create complex shapes, ideal for components like engine blocks. Extrusion shapes the alloy by forcing it through a die, producing continuous profiles used in construction and automotive applications. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping the alloy through mechanical force, enhancing strength and durability, suitable for high-stress applications. Each type serves distinct industrial needs based on structural requirements.</p></p>
